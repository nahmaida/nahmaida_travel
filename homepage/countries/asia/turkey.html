Turkey

Turkey is a country that straddles Eastern Europe and Western Asia with cultural connections to ancient Greek, Persian, Roman, Byzantine, and Ottoman empires. Its rich history is reflected in its bustling cities, stunning landscapes, and numerous historical sites.

Istanbul

Istanbul, formerly known as Byzantium and Constantinople, is a major city in Turkey that straddles Europe and Asia across the Bosphorus Strait. Its rich history includes influences from various civilizations, and it is known for its vibrant markets, historic sites, and cultural diversity.

Bosphorus

The Bosphorus Strait, a natural waterway that divides Istanbul, is a significant shipping route and a scenic landmark that connects the Black Sea with the Sea of Marmara.

Walls of Constantinople

The Walls of Constantinople are a series of stone walls that have surrounded and protected the city of Constantinople (now Istanbul) since its founding as the capital of the Roman Empire by Constantine the Great.

Basilica Cistern

The Basilica Cistern is one of the largest ancient cisterns beneath Istanbul, Turkey, built in the 6th century during the reign of Byzantine Emperor Justinian I. It is a marvel of engineering and architecture.

Hagia Sophia

Hagia Sophia, also known as Ayasofya, is a historic mosque and former Greek Orthodox Christian basilica and later an Ottoman imperial mosque and now a museum. It is renowned for its massive dome and beautiful architecture, symbolizing the rich history of Istanbul.
